Turnitin

Overview

Turnitin is a piece of software integrated within NOW (our VLE) used to detect plagiarism in student work.

Learning Outcomes

  1. Recall what Turnitin is used for
  2. Identify how its use can support academic integrity

Information

Turnitin is a service that compares student submitted work against a database of millions of previous student submissions, a range of academic books and journals, and internet sources. It highlights instances where student work is similar to existing work and links to the original sources.

The core functionality of Turnitin encourages original work, investigates authorship, and deters students from submitting assignments that compromise NTU’s integrity.

The Turnitin similarity reports can be used by students to support the development of their academic writing by identifying development areas in referencing, paraphrasing and summarising literature.

While some schools may mark and grade students' work in NOW, you can mark and provide feedback through Turnitin's Feedback Studio. Feedback Studio features inline annotation (including pre-populated and editable Quick Marks), audio feedback, and rubrics to support grading. (NB: Feedback Studio rubrics does not currently support Grade-Based Assessment, NTU's grading strategy)

For instructions on how to set-up an assignment with Turnitin, please read the Turnitin Guide for Staff.
